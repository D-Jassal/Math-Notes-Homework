\stepcounter{section}
\section*{Problem 1}
Let
\[
    A=
    \begin{bmatrix}
        2&1&1&1\\
        1&3&3&1\\
        1&0&1&2\\
        0&1&1&3
    \end{bmatrix}.
\]
Find a (psuedo) rational form of $A$. That is find a matrix $B$ such that $B^{-1}AB$ has the form
\[
    B^{-1}AB=
    \begin{bmatrix}
        0&0&0&-c_0\\
        1&0&0&-c_1\\
        0&1&0&-c_2\\
        0&0&1&-c_3
    \end{bmatrix},
\]
and explain what $c_0,c_1,c_2,c_3$ are. Software is allowed.

\stepcounter{section}
\section*{Problem 2}
Let
\[
    A=
    \begin{bmatrix}
        11&-1&-5&-1\\
        -1&11&-1&-5\\
        -5&-1&11&-1\\
        -1&-5&-1&11
    \end{bmatrix}.
\]
Find an orthogonal matrix $Q$ such that $Q^TAQ$ is diagonal. Use of sotware is recommended.

\stepcounter{section}
\section*{Problem 3}
Determine is 
\[
    A=
    \begin{bmatrix}
        1&1&0\\
        0&1&1\\
        1&0&1
    \end{bmatrix}
\]
is unitarily diagonalizable.

\stepcounter{section}
\section*{Problem 4}
Prove that real skew-symmetric matrices are unitarily diagonalizable. Recall $A\in M_n(\R)$ is called skew-symmetric if $A^T=-A$.
