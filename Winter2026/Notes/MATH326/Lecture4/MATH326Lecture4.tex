\subsection{Finding the Basis of a Span}
Let $U=\mathrm{span}\{v_1,v_2,\dots,v_n\}.$ Find a basis for $U$.
\subsection*{Method 1: Removing unnecessary vectors}
Steps:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Make a matrix $A$, whose columns are the vectors $v_1,v_2,\dots v_n.$
    \item Find r.r.e.f($A$).
    \item Vectors $v_i$ with $i$ corresponding to vectors of r.r.e.f($A$) containing leading 1's (pivot vectors) form a baiss of $\mathrm{col}(A)=\mathrm{span}(v_1,v_2,\dots v_n).$
\end{enumerate}
This works because the linear relations of $v_1,v_2,\dots v_n$ are preserved in r.r.e.f($A$).
\[
    \mathrm{r.r.e.f}(A)=UA.
\]
Where $U$ is an invertible matrix made of elementary matrices.
\begin{align*}
    c_1v_1+c_2v_2+\cdots+c_nv_n=0&\Rightarrow U(c_1v_1+c_2v_2+\cdots+c_nv_n)=0\\
    &=c_1(Uv_1)+c_2(Uv_2)+\cdots+c_n(Uv_n)=0.
\end{align*}
\[
    \mathrm{r.r.e.f}(A)=UA=[Uv_1\dots uv_n].
\]
So the columns of r.r.e.f($A$) are lienarly realted to the columns of $A$ and vice versa.
\subsection*{Method2: ``Pretty'' Basis}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Make a matric whose rows are the given vectors
    $\begin{bmatrix}
        \noindent\rule{.5cm}{0.4pt}&v_1&\noindent\rule{.5cm}{0.4pt}\\
        \noindent\rule{.5cm}{0.4pt}&v_2&\noindent\rule{.5cm}{0.4pt}\\
        &\vdots&\\
        \noindent\rule{.5cm}{0.4pt}&v_n&\noindent\rule{.5cm}{0.4pt}\\
    \end{bmatrix}$
    \item Find r.r.e.f($A^T$). The non-zero vectors of $A^T$ form a basis of $\mathrm{row}(A^T)=\mathrm{col}(A)$.
\end{enumerate}
To complete a given set to a basis of a space say $\R^n$, add the vectors $e_1,e_2,\dots,e_n$ and perform one of the methods to find a basis.
