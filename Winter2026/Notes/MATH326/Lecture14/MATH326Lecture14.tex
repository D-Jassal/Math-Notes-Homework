Pivot decomposition is quite literally the same as what we know as full rank decomposition.\\
Moreover Pivot columns of $A$ times rref$A=A$. $R=$rref$A$, $P=$ pivot columns of $A$. The columns of $P$ are a basis of col$(A)$ and rows of $R$ are a basis of row$(A)$.
\lemp{(Theorem 3.1.22 in the book)}{
    Let $A,B\in M_{n\times k}$ and let both have full column rank, that is $\rank(A)=\rank(B)=k$. Then there exists a matrix $C\in M_{m\times m}$ such that 
    \[
        B=CA.
    \]
}{
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item[Case 1.] $k=m$ then $A,B$ are invertible, then $C=(A^{-1}B)$.
        \item[Case 2.] $1\geq k<m$. Add $m-k$ columns to $A$ that are linearly independant. The matrix is 
        \[
            [A\,\,X]_{m\times n}
        \] 
        then $A$ becomes a basis of $F^n$. Do the same to $B$ making $[B\,\,Y]$, completing it to a basis.
        Let 
        \begin{align*}
            C&=[B\,\,Y][A\,\,X]^{-1}\\
            [CA\,\,CX]&=[B\,\,Y]
        \end{align*} 
        then we have the desired result.
    \end{enumerate}
}
\subsection*{Row Equivalence of Matrices}
\defn{Row Equivalence}{
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Let $A,B\in M_{m\times n}$. We say that they are row equivalent if row$(A)=$row($B$).
        \item Let $A,B\in M_{m\times n}$. We say they are left equivalent if there exists $C\in M_{m\times m}$ that is invertible such that $B=CA$.
        \item Let $A,B\in M_{m\times n}$. We say they are right equivalent if there exists $C\in M_{m\times m}$ that is invertible such that $B=AC$.
    \end{enumerate}
}
\propp{
    Let $A,B\in M_{m\times n}$ then the following statements are equivalent.
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item $A,B$ are row equivalent;
        \item $A,B$ are left equivalent;
        \item $\null(A)=\null(B)$;
        \item rref$(A)=$rref($B$);
        \item $A=PR$ and $B=CR$ are the pivot column decompositions.
    \end{enumerate}
}{
    $\row(A)=\row(B)$, $A=PR$ and $B=QR$, so $P$ and $Q$ have full rank and there by the lemme there exists an invertible $C$ such that $CP=Q\Rightarrow CA=B$.\\
    $(2)\Rightarrow(1)$ 
}
